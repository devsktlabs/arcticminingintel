Understanding NI 43-101 and Competent Persons Reports

By Intel Desk · 2026-03-23

NI 43-101CPRresource reportingmining standards

When a mining company reports a mineral resource or reserve, the numbers must comply with specific reporting standards. Understanding these standards is essential for evaluating the reliability of reported resources. This article explains the key reporting frameworks relevant to Greenland mining investments.

What Are Resource Reporting Standards?

Resource reporting standards are regulatory frameworks that define:

  • **How mineral resources and reserves are estimated and reported**
  • **Who is qualified to prepare and sign off on resource estimates**
  • **What information must be disclosed alongside resource figures**
  • **The difference between resources and reserves**

    • These standards exist to protect investors by ensuring that mineral resource disclosures are transparent, consistent, and reliable.

    NI 43-101 (Canada)

    The National Instrument 43-101 (NI 43-101) is the most widely recognized resource reporting standard in the mining industry:

  • **Jurisdiction**: Required for mining companies listed on Canadian stock exchanges (TSX, TSXV). Widely adopted as a best practice globally
  • **Qualified Person (QP)**: Resource estimates must be prepared and signed by a QP, who is an engineer or geoscientist with at least 5 years of relevant experience
  • **Technical report**: Any public disclosure of a new material resource estimate must be accompanied by a technical report within 45 days
  • **Resource categories**: NI 43-101 defines Inferred, Indicated, and Measured resource categories, and Proven and Probable reserve categories
  • **Applicability to Greenland**: While Greenland is not a Canadian jurisdiction, GRML (as a NASDAQ-listed company) would typically adopt NI 43-101 standards or equivalent for resource reporting

    JORC Code (Australia)

    The Joint Ore Reserves Committee (JORC) Code is the Australian equivalent of NI 43-101:

  • **Jurisdiction**: Required for mining companies listed on the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX)
  • **Competent Person**: Similar to NI 43-101 QP. Must be a member of a recognized professional organization (e.g., AusIMM, AIG)
  • **Resource categories**: Inferred, Indicated, and Measured (similar to NI 43-101)

    • Competent Persons Report (CPR) / PERC (UK/Europe)

    The PERC (Pan-European Reserves and Resources Reporting Committee) Code is used in Europe:

  • **Jurisdiction**: Recognized by the London Stock Exchange and other European exchanges
  • **Competent Person**: Similar qualifications to NI 43-101 QP
  • **Resource categories**: Aligned with CRIRSCO (Committee for Mineral Reserves International Reporting Standards) template

    • United States (SEC)

    The US SEC has historically had less detailed mining disclosure requirements but has been updating its framework:

  • **SEC Industry Guide 7**: The traditional US standard, which is less detailed than NI 43-101
  • **SEC Modernization (2020)**: New rules adopted by the SEC to align more closely with international standards (including recognition of Inferred resources)
  • **Applicability to GRML**: As a NASDAQ-listed company, GRML must comply with SEC reporting requirements. Adoption of NI 43-101 or equivalent standards is expected

    • CRIRSCO: The International Framework

    The Committee for Mineral Reserves International Reporting Standards (CRIRSCO) is the umbrella organization that coordinates national reporting codes:

  • **Members**: Canada (CIM), Australia (JORC), South Africa (SAMREC), UK/Europe (PERC), USA (SME), Chile, and others
  • **Alignment**: All CRIRSCO-member codes are broadly aligned on resource and reserve definitions, ensuring international consistency

    • Resource Categories Explained

    Mineral Resource: A concentration of material in or on the Earth's crust with reasonable prospects for eventual economic extraction.

  • **Inferred**: Lowest confidence. Based on limited drilling and geological inference. Not reliable enough for detailed mine planning. Typically assigned 0-50% conversion to reserves
  • **Indicated**: Moderate confidence. Based on sufficient drilling to assume continuity. Suitable for preliminary mine planning. Typically assigned 50-70% conversion to reserves
  • **Measured**: Highest confidence. Based on closely spaced drilling with confirmed continuity. Suitable for detailed mine planning. Typically assigned 70-90% conversion to reserves

    • Mineral Reserve: The economically mineable part of a Measured or Indicated resource.

  • **Probable**: Based on Indicated resources (and sometimes Measured). Lower confidence than Proven. Suitable for mine planning with some uncertainty
  • **Proven**: Based on Measured resources. Highest confidence. Suitable for detailed mine planning

    • Key distinction: A resource is an estimate of what is in the ground. A reserve is the portion of the resource that can be economically extracted. All reserves are resources, but not all resources are reserves.

    Skaergaard Resource Status

    Skaergaard's reported resource of 25.4M oz PdEq + 23.5M oz AuEq is classified as combined indicated and inferred (per NI 43-101, SLR Consulting, Nov 2022). This means:

  • **Confidence level**: Moderate. The resource is based on sufficient drilling to assume geological and grade continuity
  • **Reliability**: Suitable for preliminary economic assessment and mine planning
  • **Next step**: Further drilling (particularly infill drilling) will upgrade portions of the resource to Measured category, supporting a Proven reserve estimate in the feasibility study
  • **No reserves yet**: A reserve cannot be declared until a feasibility study demonstrates economic viability

    • Red Flags in Resource Reporting

    Investors should watch for:

  • **Heavy reliance on Inferred resources**: High Inferred percentages indicate low confidence. These are speculative by nature
  • **Outdated resource estimates**: Resources should be updated as new drilling data becomes available
  • **Unqualified resource estimates**: Any resource estimate not prepared and signed by a QP/CP is unreliable
  • **Missing technical reports**: Technical reports should be available for public review
  • **Unrealistic mining assumptions**: Pay attention to mining methods, cut-off grades, and recovery assumptions in the resource estimate

    • Conclusion

    Mineral resource reporting standards exist to protect investors by ensuring transparency and reliability in reported resources. For Skaergaard, the Indicated resource classification provides moderate confidence, and further drilling will upgrade portions to Measured category as the project advances toward feasibility. Investors should always review the underlying technical report and understand the resource categories and assumptions behind any reported resource figure.
